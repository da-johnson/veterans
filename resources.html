  • Warrior-Scholar Project: Official Site
    • Warrior-Scholar Project's vision is to ensure every degree-seeking enlisted veteran and transitioning service member thrives in their pursuit of higher education and beyond.
    • Mission: The organization aims to drive enlisted veterans and service members towards success in higher education and beyond via transformative programming that enhances skills, boosts confidence, and cultivates lasting connections.
    • What They Do: In collaboration with top-notch colleges and universities, Warrior-Scholar Project organizes intense, immersive one and two-week academic boot camps in three disciplines: Humanities, STEM, and Business & Entrepreneurship. These boot camps provide enlisted veterans and transitioning service members with the tools to excel in higher education and smoothen their shift from military service to academia. Additionally, the organization offers workshops specially crafted for community college-enrolled veterans and tailored programs for marginalized veterans, including the Women Veterans Empowerment Dialogues. All of these programs are offered at no charge.
    • Beliefs: WSP believes in fostering an inclusive environment and offering bridging programs to ensure veterans' success in higher education. The diverse experiences of enlisted service members and veterans endow them with distinct skills, preparing them for success in higher education and beyond. WSP stands by the belief that every veteran should have access to higher education and remains dedicated to bridging the educational disparity faced by minority service members and veterans.
  • NSF 23-161 Dear Colleague Letter: Veterans Research Supplement (VRS) Program
    • Open to veteran STEM students, both undergraduate and graduate, from U.S. 2- and 4-year educational institutions.
    • Graduate students seeking support should not have other government-backed funding, including from Principal Investigators on NSF awards or NSF Graduate Research Fellowships. However, those who've utilized the Post-9/11 GI Bill and similar programs can apply.
    • The graduate VRS funding mainly covers stipends, benefits, tuition, and certain indirect costs. Support is initially granted for one year, extendable up to three years based on the research award duration and the student's progress. The student's impact and progress must be detailed in annual reports. Renewals depend on available funding.
  • Service to School: Service to School (S2S)
    • Service to School (S2S) is a 501(c)(3) non-profit that provides free college and grad school application counseling to military veterans and servicemembers.
    • Their goal is to help veterans gain admission to the best college for them while empowering each veteran to make informed decisions about their education.
  • Pathway to Science:Official Site
    • The mission of the Institute for Broadening Participation is to increase diversity in the Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) workforce. Since 2003, IBP has connected underrepresented students with STEM funding and research opportunities, and has provided faculty and administrators with tools and resources to help promote the positive factors that keep underrepresented students on the STEM pathway into successful STEM careers. They design and implement strategies to increase access to STEM education, funding, and careers, with special emphasis on diverse underrepresented groups. They believe that diversifying the STEM workforce is the best way to ensure our nation's economic vitality and solve global challenges.
  • Caltech Program for Veterans: CIAN's Research Opportunity
    • CIAN welcomes veteran students (undergraduate and graduate) of the United States Military into our labs during the academic year and summer to participate in research that is creating ultra-broadband communication for the next generation internet. Participants will be given consultation and financial support to obtain real hands-on research experience in optical communications, thus preparing or expanding the career potential for participants both in the classroom and in the workforce.
  • Yale's Program for Veterans: Research Experience for Veteran Undergraduates
    • The Research Experience for Veteran Undergraduates is an intense, 9-week summer program hosted by Yale University where enlisted U.S. veteran undergraduate students perform research in a STEM field of their interest and develop skills necessary to become a research scientist.
